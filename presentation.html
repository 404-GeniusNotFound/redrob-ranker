Solution Overview

What is the Proposed Solution?

A 5-stage pipeline that replaces keyword matching with career-narrative analysis. Instead of counting keyword hits, the system reads a candidate's entire career story — where they worked, what they built, how long they stayed — and scores them across 6 orthogonal dimensions calibrated to this specific JD.

What Differentiates This Approach?

  • Career coherence scoring — penalizes all-consulting backgrounds and title-chasers (< 18mo avg tenure), rewards product-company depth
  • Skill authenticity checks — cross-references claimed skills against career descriptions (listing FAISS without ever mentioning search = suspicious)
  • Behavioral signal fusion — treats response rate, recency, and notice period as 15% of the total score (most teams ignore this)
  • 6-check honeypot detector — catches impossible profiles before they contaminate rankings
  • No LLM calls — runs in 68s on CPU using TF-IDF with trigrams

Candidate Signals Beyond Keywords

  • Career description text — what they actually built, not what they list as skills
  • Proficiency × duration — "expert" in TensorFlow for 36 months > "expert" for 2 months
  • Company type analysis — product companies (Google, Amazon) vs consulting firms (TCS, Infosys)
  • Role progression — IC → Senior → Staff in tech track is positive; lateral moves across non-tech roles is negative
  • Platform engagement — response rate, last-active recency, interview completion rate
  • Skill-career corroboration — do their career descriptions actually mention the skills they claim?

How Signals Are Combined

  • Each dimension scores 0 to max points (25, 25, 20, 15, 10, 5)
  • Normalized to 0–1 range, then multiplied by weight
  • final = Σ(dim_score / max_pts × weight)
  • JD alignment uses TF-IDF with sim^0.6 stretch to separate top candidates

Models & Algorithms Used

  • TF-IDF — scikit-learn vectorizer with trigrams (1,3) for semantic matching
  • Cosine similarity — candidate text vs JD reference vector
  • Rule-based scoring — handcrafted for this specific JD's requirements
  • No ML model training — all weights are JD-derived, not data-fitted

Explainability & Data Validation

How Rankings Are Explained

Each candidate gets a 1-2 sentence reasoning using 5 rotating patterns:

Rank #2 (score: 0.8025)

"6-year career spanning Genpact AI, LinkedIn; demonstrates production embeddings & retrieval experience and including product-company experience."

Rank #42 (score: 0.7208)

"Strong career coherence profile — 11 years with depth in OpenCV, JavaScript, Go; matches the JD need for Python & ML proficiency. Note: not currently marked open to work."

Rank #92 (score: 0.6913)

"7-year career spanning Freshworks, Netflix; demonstrates production embeddings experience. Caveat: borderline fit — 90-day notice period."

Honeypot Detection (6 Checks)

1Exp-Date MismatchClaims 10yr, career totals 4yr

3Title-Desc MismatchHR title but ML descriptions
4Impossible Timeline60mo role spanning 12mo

6Perfect SignalsAll 1.0 scores on suspicious profile

Result: 479 honeypots detected out of 92,530 scored candidates. 0 honeypots in top 100

Hallucination Prevention

  • Reasoning only references fields present in the candidate dict
  • Skills, companies, titles extracted directly — never generated
  • 100/100 reasonings verified unique
